Rules

Please note: these summer leagues are Just For Fun! They are organised for those who want to keep playing singles during the months when there’s less rain and more daylight. There are no prizes for winning and we like to keep it social. See you in the bar!

Arranging games

The boxleague has been created with each match given a scheduled date slot. The scheduled date is a preferred date only and you can of course play your match at any time - just contact your opponent to agree the actual date and time that suits you both. After you have played your match, agree which of you will enter the score in the Scoreboard.

Player contact information is displayed in your box (see My box Players).

Player becomes unavailable for the boxleague (e.g. due to injury or otherwise)

Players can mark themselves as unavailable (i.e. available "no") for the duration of the league by going to Settings, updating the available flag to "no" and saving.

Prior games played by a player unavailable will not be calculated in the final score. All games by and vs the player will not count.

Player unable to play (e.g. no time available)

If your opponent cannot offer you many dates to play (through being busy, rather than unavailable for the boxleague) then that’s OK because It’s Just For Fun. However, to keep the scores working, you can agree with your opponent to a WALKOVER for the match. You will score 10 points, your opponent 0.

In this event, no sets or games will count towards your total, you will only receive points.

Player no show on arranged time and date

If your opponent fails to turn up at the agreed time and date then you can agree with your opponent to a WALKOVER for the match. You will score 10 points, your opponent 0. But let’s try not to let this happen!

In this event, no sets or games will count towards your total, you will only receive points.

Player unable to complete game (e.g. due to injury or otherwise)

If your opponent cannot continue to play a current game then you can agree a CONCEDE for the match. The points scored will be equivalent to you winning the remainder of the sets.

Only completed sets and games will count towards your total, you will receive points accordingly.

Promotion and demotion

The top two players on the boxleague's end date will be promoted up and the bottom two will be demoted down. The ranking is calculated in the following order: points(Pts), then sets difference(SD), then game difference(GD).

New joiners in leagues above and or players retiring may affect the final box placing. Your promotion is therefore relative to your peers and not the final boxleague.

Have fun and enjoy the summer tennis!
