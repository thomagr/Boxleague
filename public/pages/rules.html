Rules

Arranging games

Your boxleague has been created with each match given a scheduled date slot. The scheduled date is a preferred date only and you should contact your opponent to agree the actual date and time of your match.
After you have played your match you should agree the one player to enter the match score. The other player should check a correct entry.

Player contact information is available in your box (see My box Players).

Player becomes unavailable for the boxleague (e.g. due to injury or otherwise)

Players may mark themselves as not available for the duration of the league by going to Settings and updating the available flag to unchecked.

Prior games played by an unavailable player will not be calculated in the final score. All games by and vs an unavailable player will not count.

Player unable to play (e.g. no time avilable)

If your opponent cannot offer you many dates to play (through being busy, rather than unavailable for the boxleague) then you can agree to a WALKOVER for the match. You will score 10 points, your opponent 0.

No sets or games will count towards your total, you will only receive points.

Player no show on arranged time and date

If your opponent fails to turn up at the agreed time and date then you can agree to a WALKOVER for the match. You will score 10 points, your opponent 0.

No sets or games will count towards your total, you will only receive points.

Player unable to complete game (e.g. due to injury or otherwise)

If your opponent cannot continue to play a current game then you can agree a CONCEDE for the match. The points scored will be equivalent to you winning the remainder of the sets.
A concede could result in both players awarded the same or close points (i.e. 12 for 7:5 5:7 ), in these situations the conceding player will be docked a further 2 points.

Only completed sets and games will count towards your total.

Promotion and demotion

The top two players on the boxleague's end date will be promoted up and the bottom two will be demoted down. The ranking is calculated in the following order: points(Pts), then sets difference(SD), then game difference(GD).

Old Rules

  1. You must turn up to play as scheduled on the fixture list UNLESS you contact your opponent and re-arrange it, which you are welcome to do. Let’s not have any no- shows.
  2. A NO-SHOW is when you turn up as scheduled or arranged and your opponent fails to turn up. Highly annoying! The player giving the walkover gets 0 points for the match and the winner (ie the player being given the walkover) gets 10 points. Please email the League Organiser if this happens and explain the circumstances.
  3. Matches will be either two tie break sets and a 10 point match tie break at one set all OR three tie break sets. Agree the format at the start of each match.
  4. You are expected to play all 5 matches over the 6 week period. The minimum commitment to staying in the competition is 3 matches per round.
  5. If you are injured and cannot play, contact your opponents to let them and copy the Organiser in. Injured players will be placed on the waiting list for future rounds.
  6. If your opponent cannot offer you many dates to play (through being busy, rather than injured) then you can ask them to CONCEDE the match. Let the Organiser know by e- mail and you will score 10 points, your opponent 0.
  7. Promotion and demotion is decided on points. In principle top two go up, bottom two go down but new joiners in leagues above can mean that this is not always the case.
  8. If a player is injured during a match the result is based on the number of games/sets finished before retirement. For example, at 5/6 in the first set. The winner will get 18 points and the loser will get a point per game they won eg 6 if this is the player that is injured and can’t complete the match. An injured player who can’t complete their matches will have the scores for the completed matches kept but no further points will be allocated for those who haven’t been able to play the injured player.
